Shelter and Location

A spatial analysis of the distribution of low income housing near
light rail stations in Seattle, WA.


Capstone Project by Alan Foote

In 2009, Sound Transit began operation of the Link Light Rail service that connected downtown Seattle to the regional airport through the city’s most historically underserved communities in its South end. To aid in the city’s evolving affordable housing crisis, the areas around the light rail stations were rezoned to enable transit-oriented development (TOD), and in 2014 a committee was created to produce the Mandatory Housing Affordability (MHA) act of 2019 which requires the creation of low-income housing in new mixed-use structures within the city. However, as the third phase of the Link moves further outside the city boundaries some areas around Link stations are still lacking the promoted construction of affordable and equitable transit-oriented development (eTOD) that has been encouraged and incentivized to be created. In a city with stark housing inequalities, a shrinking supply of low-income housing, and a purportedly progressive housing policy these issues are often overlooked by the absence of visualizations to influence decision making.
